Encebollado

Description

This dish is a very popular soup dish in Ecuador, specifically the coast area. I grew up eating this soup dish because my mom only cooked ecuadorian food. Its a warm and comforting dish that can be eaten at any time of the day. This dish is made of just a coupke of ingredients. Its simple, I think anybody can make it!

Ingredients

  • About 1 liter of Water
  • 2 lb of Fresh Tuna cut into bite sized pieces.
  • 1 lb of Yuca chopped into large chunks
  • 2 red Onions sliced
  • 1 red onion cut into 4
  • 2 Tomato sliced
  • 1 tomato cut into 4
  • 2 Green peppers cut into 4
  • 1 Head of Cilantro chopped, stems seperated and diced finely
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • About 4-6 Limes
  • 4 tbs of Garlic powder
  • 4 tbs of cumin powder
  • 3 tbs of aji peruano (peruvian pepper powder)

Instructions

  1. Place your red onion, green pepper, and tomato that have been cut into 4 into a blender with 1/4 cup of water.Blend till it forms a semi chunky liquid.
  2. Heat a large pot with water. Once water is simmering, add the mixture from the blender. Add cilantro stems.Add in your spices.
  3. Bring large pot to a boil and add in your yuca.
  4. Let the yuca boil unitl it is soft enough to stick a fork through.
  5. While the yuca is cooking, you'll be making the "salsa". Slice up your tomatos and onions. Place onions into a bowl with 2 tbs of salt and enough water to cover the onions. This is to remove some of that sour onion taste.
  6. Drain the onoions after about 5 minutes and mix with the sliced tomatoes. Finely chop your head of cilantro and add to the onions and tomatos.
  7. Squeeze about 4 limes into the "salsa". Add salt to taste.
  8. Once the yuca is ready, take it out of the pot and set aside into a bowl. Let it cool for a couple of minutes. Cut the yuca into bite sized pieces, removing the middle stem.
  9. Add the tuna and cook until its almost fully cooked
  10. Now, add the yuca. let that cook until the tuna is fully cooked >
  11. Once evrything is fully cooked. Serve in bowl with "salsa" on top.
  12. You can eat this soup with a side of rice, some chifles (thinly sliced plantains), toasted corn nuts, some pickled spicy vegetable, or some lemons.
